Workout Plan

Strength & Power
Fewer reps · real rest
Lower body · power → heavy → light · ~20 min
Warm-up · 10 leg swings/leg · 15 slow squats · 10 walking lunges
1
Squat Jumpsor fast stand-ups
4 × 5rest 60–90sBodyweight
Power, done fresh: explode up, soft landing, full reset each rep.
Feet shoulder-width. Squat to parallel, then drive explosively upward. Land softly with knees slightly bent and fully reset before the next rep.
2
Bulgarian Split Squatsrear foot raised
3 × 8 /legrest 60sset 8–12kg
3-sec descent, shin vertical, weight in front heel.
Rest your rear foot on a chair behind you, front foot about a stride ahead. Lower your back knee toward the floor, keeping your front shin vertical. Drive through your front heel to stand.
3
Romanian Deadliftsdumbbells · heaviest
3 × 10rest 60sbump to 10–16kg
Flat back always — never round the spine. Push your backside back, hinge at the hips, soft knees. Go light while your back settles and master the hinge first (see the Back tab).
Hold DBs in front of your thighs. Push your hips back and hinge forward with a flat back — stop when you feel a hamstring stretch. Drive hips forward to stand, squeeze glutes at the top. If the lower back feels it at all, go lighter.
4
Single-Leg Calf Raisesoff a step
2 × 12 /legrest 45sBW or hold 1 DB
Full range, hold wall for balance.
Stand on one foot on the edge of a step, heel hanging off. Lower your heel below the step, then rise up onto your tiptoe as high as possible. Touch the wall lightly for balance only.
5
Wall Sitoptional finisher
2 × 30–45sBodyweight
The only burn bit — keep it short.
Back flat against a wall, feet out in front. Slide down until thighs are parallel to the floor and knees are at 90°. Hold still — don't let the back peel off the wall.
Core finisher · optional · Plank 2 × 30s · Dead bug 2 × 8/side. Brace as if bracing for a punch; don't let the lower back arch.
Chest · arms · back-safe · weight only goes down · ~25–30 min
Warm-up · arm circles · 15 band pull-aparts · 10 slow push-ups
1
Explosive Push-upspush off the floor fast
4 × 5rest 60–90sBodyweight
Power, done fresh: hold a rigid plank, hips up, don't let the lower back sag. Knees down if needed.
Hands just wider than shoulders, body in a straight line from head to heels. Lower chest to the floor with control, then drive up as fast as possible — hands can leave the floor. Reset fully between reps.
2
DB Floor / Bench Pressmain lift · heaviest
3 × 8rest 60sset 12–20kg
Control to chest, drive up. Lying down keeps the back supported.
Lie on your back (on floor or bench), DBs at chest height with elbows at roughly 45°. Press up until arms are extended, then lower slowly back until DBs touch your chest.
3
DB Seated Shoulder Pressback against wall or bench
3 × 8rest 60sdial to 8–14kg
Sit with your back fully supported against a wall or bench — removes the lower-back arch entirely. Ribs down, press straight up.
Sit on a chair or the floor with your back flat against a wall. Hold DBs at shoulder height, palms forward. Brace your core and press straight overhead. Lower back to shoulders with control — the wall support means no lumbar arch.
4
DB Floor Flylying · chest stretch
3 × 10rest 60s4–10kg
Soft elbows, open wide for a chest stretch, squeeze back together. Back stays flat on the floor.
Lie on your back, DBs above your chest with a slight bend in the elbows. Open arms wide and lower until you feel a stretch across the chest, then squeeze the chest to bring them back together. Do not let the lower back arch off the floor.
5
Lying Triceps Extensionfloor · elbows up
3 × 10rest 45s4–10kg
Elbows pointing straight up, lower toward your forehead, extend. Back stays flat on the floor.
Lie on the floor, DBs held above your forehead with elbows pointing up. Bend only at the elbows to lower the DBs toward your forehead, then extend back up. Keep the upper arms still and the lower back pressed flat throughout.
6
DB Lateral Raisesshoulder finisher · optional
2 × 15rest 45s2–6kg light
Lead with elbows, slow, no swinging. Trim this first if short on time.
Hold DBs at your sides with a slight bend in the elbows. Raise both arms out to the sides to shoulder height, leading with your elbows. Lower slowly — use a very light weight and avoid shrugging or swinging.
Core finisher · optional · Dead bug 2 × 8/side · Plank 2 × 30s. Press the lower back flat to the floor on the dead bug.
Back · rear delts · biceps · dumbbells first, then bands · ~20 min
Warm-up · 15 band pull-aparts · arm swings · shoulder rolls
1
Fast Dumbbell Rowschest on sofa back or braced hinge
4 × 5rest 60–90sset 10–16kg
Back-safe default: rest your chest on the back of a sofa to support the spine, or hinge with a hard-braced flat back. Start light. Pull fast to the ribs, control the lower.
Option A (safest): drape your chest over the back of a sofa, arms hanging down, and row both DBs up to your ribs fast. Option B: hinge forward with knees soft and back locked flat, brace hard, then pull. Never round the lower back.
2
One-Arm DB Rowhand on chair · heaviest
3 × 8 /armrest 60sbump to 14–24kg
Main horizontal pull — your strongest. Pull to the hip, flat back. Then set the DBs down.
Place one hand and the same-side knee on a chair for support. Hold DB in the opposite hand, arm hanging down. Pull the DB up to your hip, keeping your back flat and elbow close to your body. Lower slowly.
3
Banded Lat Pulldownband + door anchor, kneeling
3 × 12rest 60sBand
Vertical pull — pull elbows down to your ribs, control the stretch up.
Anchor the band at the top of a door. Kneel facing the door and grip the band with both hands. Pull your elbows down and back toward your ribs, squeezing your lats at the bottom. Slowly let the band pull your arms back up.
4
Band Face Pullsposture & rear delts
2 × 15rest 45sBand
Pull to your face, elbows high. The best move here for rounded shoulders.
Anchor band at face height. Grip with both hands, palms facing down. Pull toward your face with elbows flaring high and wide, finishing with hands beside your ears. Squeeze shoulder blades together at the end.
5
Dumbbell Curlsoptional finisher
2 × 124–10kg
Slow, no swinging — keep the elbows still. Grab the DBs again only if you fancy it.
Hold DBs at your sides, palms facing forward. Curl both up by bending at the elbows, keeping your upper arms pinned to your sides. Lower slowly back to the start.
Core finisher · optional · Bird-dog 2 × 8/side · Side plank 2 × 20s/side. Slow and controlled — this one's all posture.
