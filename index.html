Push through your heels and lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders, squeezing the glutes hard at the top — tuck the pelvis slightly so the lower back stays flat, never arched. Lower with control. To progress, rest your upper back on a sofa or bench (hip thrust) for a bigger range of motion.

Option A (safest): drape your chest over the back of a sofa, arms hanging down, and row both DBs up to your ribs fast. Option B: hinge forward with knees soft and back locked flat, brace hard, then pull. Never round the lower back.

Lie on your back on the floor or a bench, holding one dumbbell in both hands above your chest with arms only slightly bent. Keeping that slight bend, lower the weight back over your head until you feel a stretch through the lats and chest, then pull it back over your chest. Keep the ribs down and the lower back flat — don't let it arch.

Core finisher · every session · Bird-dog 2 × 8/side · Side plank 2 × 20s/side. Slow and controlled — this one's all posture.
Standing default · ribs down · pelvis tucked a touch · weight even through both feet.
Gentle off-day · spine, hips & glutes · all bodyweight · ~12 min
Note · Safe to do daily. Move slowly — this is mobility and motor control, not a workout. Two parts: spine & core first, then hips & glutes.
Part 1 · Spine & core
1
Cat-Cowspine mobilisation
8 slow
Move through the full range of the spine — don't rush.
On hands and knees. Inhale and let the belly drop (cow — arch the back). Exhale and round the spine up toward the ceiling (cat). Move slowly and breathe through each rep.
2
Glute Bridgefloor · hips up
2 × 12
Squeeze the glutes to lift, not the lower back. You can add a DB on the hips later.
Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Push through your heels and squeeze your glutes to lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Lower slowly. Do not hyperextend at the top.
3
Bird-Dogopposite arm + leg
2 × 8 /side
Keep your hips level — don't let them rotate or drop.
On hands and knees, spine neutral. Extend one arm forward and the opposite leg back at the same time, hold for 2 seconds, then return. Switch sides. Move slowly and brace the core throughout.
4
Side Plankhips off the floor
2 × 20s /side
Lift hips to a straight line. Drop to knees if needed — quality over duration.
Lie on your side, forearm on the floor, elbow under the shoulder. Lift your hips so your body forms a straight line from head to feet (or knees). Hold steady. Switch sides.
5
Curl-Up (McGill)hands under lower back
2 × 6
Lift head and shoulders just slightly — the back stays completely still.
Lie on your back, one knee bent, one leg flat. Slide your hands under the curve of your lower back to preserve its natural arch. Brace your core and lift your head and shoulders just a few centimetres — no more. Hold 8–10 seconds, lower. The lower back must not move at all.
6
Hip Hinge Practicebroomstick drill
2 × 8
Hold a broomstick touching head, mid-back & tailbone. Push hips back, soft knees, all three points stay touching — this grooves the RDL pattern.
Stand holding a broomstick or long pole vertically along your spine with one hand at your neck and one at your lower back. Push your hips back and hinge forward, keeping the stick touching your head, the middle of your back, and your tailbone throughout. Stop when the stick loses contact or you feel a hamstring stretch. Stand back up by driving the hips forward.
Part 2 · Hips & glutes
Undoes the sitting legacy — releases tight hip flexors, wakes up lazy glutes. Do on off-days or as a warm-up before Legs. Stretch first, then activate.
1
Half-kneeling Hip Flexor Stretchfront of hip · kneeling
30–40s /sidehold 35s
Squeeze the back glute and tuck your pelvis under BEFORE you lean in. Lean forward only slightly. Feel it at the front of the kneeling-leg hip, never in your lower back.
Kneel on one knee with the other foot flat in front, both knees about 90°. Before moving, squeeze the glute of the kneeling leg and tuck your pelvis under so the lower back flattens. Holding that, shift your weight forward only slightly until you feel a stretch at the front of the kneeling-leg hip. Breathe, hold, then switch sides. Never arch the lower back to chase a bigger stretch.
2
Clamshellsside glute · glute med
12–15 /side
On your side, knees bent and stacked, heels together. Lift the top knee without rolling your hips back. Targets the side glute (glute medius).
Lie on your side with hips and shoulders stacked, knees bent about 90° and heels together. Keeping your feet touching and your top hip pointing forward — don't let the pelvis roll back — lift the top knee as far as you can. Lower with control. Keep the movement at the hip, felt in the side of the glute.
3
Glute Bridge — Activation Holdglutes fire · 3s holds
10 slow · 3s hold
3-second hold at the top with a hard glute squeeze. Keep ribs down — don't arch into it. Trains the glutes to FIRE, which holds the pelvis neutral.
Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Squeeze your glutes to lift your hips into a straight line from shoulders to knees, then hold for 3 seconds with a hard glute squeeze. Keep your ribs pulled down and the lower back flat — don't arch into the top. Lower slowly. This is about the glutes firing, not speed.
4
Standing Pelvic Tiltwall drill · neutral
10 · 3s hold
Stand against a wall. Gently flatten your lower back toward the wall by tucking the pelvis. Hold 3s, release. This rehearses your neutral standing default.
Stand with your back against a wall, feet a little forward and knees soft. Gently tilt your pelvis to flatten your lower back toward the wall — a small tuck, without pushing through your legs. Hold 3 seconds, then release back to neutral. This grooves the neutral pelvis position so it becomes your standing default.
Back-safe guardrails · The glute squeeze on the hip flexor stretch is non-negotiable — it keeps the stretch off your lower spine. Never force it by arching. Keep ribs down on the bridge. If anything pinches the lower back, ease off.
Important · Because back niggles recur, a single physio check-up is worth more than any routine here. Stop any exercise immediately if it pinches, causes sharp pain, or sends pain down a leg — and see a professional before continuing.
Weekly shape: Legs · Push · Pull · rest, once or twice through per week. This Back tab is gentle enough to do daily on rest days. For cardio, keep the easy dog walks and add a couple of hard 20s–1 min bursts (hill or stair sprints) for VO2max.
